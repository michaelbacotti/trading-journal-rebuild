About the premium subscription (not currently available)

The Trading Journal is currently a free, ad-supported publication. We are not currently accepting premium subscriptions. This page exists to document the site's position on subscriptions and to record the criteria that would justify launching a paid tier in the future.

A premium subscription would only be launched if it would fund content that the free, ad-supported model cannot. Examples would include: longer-form research reports (10,000+ words with custom data analysis), weekly video walkthroughs of specific trade setups, a private Discord for trade journal subscribers to discuss the daily setups in real time, or a structured options strategy course with weekly cohorts. None of these are currently in development.

Why the journal is free and ad-supported

The journal is free and ad-supported because the trades recorded here are the trader's actual positions. There is no asymmetry of information — readers see the same entry reasoning, the same strike selection, the same management decisions, and the same outcomes that the trader sees. Charging a subscription fee would imply that subscribers are getting something the trader is not, which is not the case. The journal's value is in the transparency of the methodology, not in exclusive access to the positions.

The ad-supported model is the only monetization that does not introduce a conflict of interest with the published content. Display advertising through Google AdSense is independent of the trade decisions; affiliate relationships are with tools we actually use; there are no paid placements or sponsored content. This structure keeps the editorial content independent of the monetization.

If we launch a premium tier

If we launch a premium tier, the criteria are: (1) the content would be materially different from what is available for free, (2) the content would justify the price point, and (3) the content would not be possible to fund through advertising at the scale required. We will publish a separate post explaining the decision, the content that would be included, and the price point. We will not announce a premium tier without a clear roadmap of what subscribers would receive and when.

The free, ad-supported journal will continue regardless of any premium tier launch. The premium tier would supplement, not replace, the free content. Existing free content will not be moved behind the paywall.
