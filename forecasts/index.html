About the forecasts

The forecast section contains periodic market outlooks for SPX, XSP, and the sector ETFs that the journal trades. Forecasts are published when the market regime or the underlying tape changes in a way that warrants a tactical update, typically one to three times per week. Each forecast includes the current spot level, the implied vol regime, the VIX term structure, the upcoming catalysts on the economic calendar, and the specific position structure being recommended (or the rationale for being flat).

Forecasts are not predictions. They are tactical views based on the conditions at the time of publication. The expected move, the probability of profit, and the max loss on any proposed structure are calculated from the live options chain at the time of writing and are subject to change as the underlying moves. Readers should not interpret a forecast as a guarantee of any particular outcome; they should interpret it as a snapshot of the trader's reasoning at a specific point in time.

How forecasts are organized

Forecasts are organized by date and tagged by regime. The regime tag (BULL, BEAR, RANGE-BOUND, VOLATILE) reflects the desk's current read on the broader tape and is updated when conditions shift. Within each forecast, the structure tag (CREDIT, DEBIT, INCOME, RANGE-BOUND, DIRECTIONAL, VOLATILITY) reflects the strategy type being recommended. The combined tag (e.g., BULL / CREDIT) tells the reader at a glance what kind of structure is appropriate in the current regime.

Each forecast links to the underlying trade journal entry when a position is actually entered. When no position is entered (the desk is flat or the setup does not justify a trade), the forecast explains why. Forecasts that are still open (position still active) are marked with a status indicator in the archive.

Reading the forecast archive

The forecast archive is a record of how the desk's tactical view evolved over time. Each entry includes the SPX level, VIX, term structure, and any open positions at the time of publication. The archive is searchable by date and by regime tag. Readers researching how the desk navigated a specific market event (FOMC meeting, CPI print, geopolitical shock) can filter the archive by date to see the desk's view on each trading day during the event.
