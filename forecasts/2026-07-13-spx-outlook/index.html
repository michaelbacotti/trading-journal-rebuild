No-Trade Note: Waiting for CPI

We are holding a flat position into Wednesday's CPI print. This is a deliberate decision based on the current market setup, not inaction.

The setup: SPX closed Monday at 5,649.25, down 0.79% on the day. VIX ticked up 8% to 12.84. Energy was bid (XLE +3.29%) on Iran-related headlines. The CBOE's expected move for the week is roughly ±85 index points — a tight range that reflects a market positioned for the CPI release, not chasing a directional thesis.

Why we are not trading into the print: Wednesday morning's CPI release is a known catalyst with binary outcome risk. Pre-event implied volatility is elevated relative to the recent range, but not high enough to justify selling premium that will decay into a vol crush if the print lands in line. If the print comes in hot, an open short-premium position gets hurt by the implied-vol spike even if the directional bias was right. If the print comes in cold, we miss the relief rally because we were hedged against a different scenario.

The right move: wait for the catalyst to resolve, then reassess. The framework for the post-CPI decision tree is below.

This Week's Expected Move

Using the current VIX of 12.84 (annualized), the expected move for the week is:

  • SPX: ±1σ = ±85 points (~±1.5%)
  • XSP: ±8.5 points (~±1.5%)
  • Probability SPX closes within 1σ of 5,649: ~68%

That expected-move range defines the no-trade zone. The implied move is large enough that opening a short-premium position here is fighting the vol crush that will follow the print.

Market Context — What We're Watching

The Energy Bid

XLE (Energy Select Sector SPDR) was up 3.29% on Monday, with Iran-related headlines cited as the catalyst. The defensive-sector bid pattern is consistent with the broader risk-off tone that has characterized the past two weeks.

For options traders, energy spikes of this size historically feed through to broader market volatility within 1-2 weeks. We are watching for confirmation that the energy move is sustained before adjusting the broader market view.

VIX Divergence

VIX was up 8% on the day despite SPX down less than 1%. That divergence — realized volatility modestly elevated while implied volatility ticks higher — is the early-warning signal that someone is buying tail protection into the CPI print. We interpret this as a confirmation that the institutional positioning is cautious, not aggressive.

Breadth Holding

Roughly 68% of S&P 500 names are above their 50-day moving average. That is down from the 75%+ readings we saw in mid-June, but it is still consistent with a BULL regime. The bull trend is intact, just less broad than it was a month ago.

What Changes the Call After CPI

We have three scenarios mapped out for Wednesday afternoon, depending on the CPI print.

Scenario 1: CPI Prints In-Line (Consensus 3.1% YoY)

If CPI lands within 0.1 percentage points of consensus (between 3.0% and 3.2%), the market likely treats it as a continuation of the current disinflation path. Implied volatility will crush after the print, and short-premium structures will become attractive again.

Post-CPI structure to consider: SPX 30-DTE bull put spread, short strike at delta 0.20 (~5% OTM). This is a defined-risk, theta-positive structure that captures the post-CPI vol crush while keeping upside open.

Scenario 2: CPI Prints Hot (>3.3% YoY)

A hot print would mean inflation is reaccelerating. The Fed's path shifts back toward higher-for-longer. Risk assets sell off, and the VIX likely spikes 4-6 points.

Post-CPI structure to consider: Wait for the vol spike to settle (typically 2-3 sessions), then consider a put-spread insurance position on SPX for any portfolio hedge that needs to be re-established. For new directional exposure, the bear-call vertical on SPX at delta 0.20 short, 30 DTE becomes the candidate if SPX 5-day return drops below -3%.

Scenario 3: CPI Prints Cold (<2.9% YoY)

A cold print would confirm disinflation and could shift Fed expectations toward earlier cuts. Rate-sensitive sectors (XLK, XLF) likely rally, and the VIX likely drops 2-4 points.

Post-CPI structure to consider: SPX 30-DTE bull put spread at delta 0.15 (~6% OTM) to capture the lower-vol regime. For a more directional play, XSP bull call vertical at delta 0.55 long, 21 DTE, sized appropriately to the higher-vol recovery.
