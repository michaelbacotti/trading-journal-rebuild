The trade. Sell the SPX $7,135 put / buy the $7,125 put, expiring Friday morning, August 21, 2026 (AM-settled — the standard monthly SPX settles on the Friday opening print, not the close). Net credit ~$130 per contract. Max loss ~$870. Breakeven $7,133.70. Probability of profit ~80% based on the ~0.18-delta short put.

Why this structure. SPX closed 0.77% lower on the week after the defensive rotation, and the term structure is in mild contango (VIX 18.77 vs. VIX3M 20.54, term ratio 0.91). That setup favors short-premium structures on a defensive bid. A bull put spread keeps the upside open — if SPX rallies through $7,135, the spread expires worthless for full credit.

Strike selection. The $7,135 short strike sits ~4.3% below spot. At SPX 19% IV and 32 calendar days to the Aug 21 expiry, that distance corresponds to a delta of roughly −0.18 on the short put. The 10-point width to the long strike creates the standard risk-defined spread.

Risks to the trade

  • AM-settlement pin risk. Because the position settles on the Friday morning opening print, any Thursday-night headline (CPI, geopolitical, Fed leak) can move SPX through the strikes before the option holder can react. The Aug 21 expiry is before the Jackson Hole symposium (Aug 21–23), so there is no Fed-event overlap, but the structure still carries the standard AM-expiry overnight risk.
  • Last trade day is Thursday Aug 20. SPX standard monthly options stop trading at the close on the trading day before expiration. You cannot manage the position on Friday morning — your exit must be in place by Thursday close.
  • Iran escalation. A Hormuz disruption that pushes Brent above $100 would likely drag SPX through the short strike. Position sizing accounts for the tail at $870 max loss per contract, but if the energy bid extends materially, consider closing early rather than holding to expiry.
  • FOMC surprise. A 50 bps cut would invert the term structure and could push SPX 1–2% on the day. Plan to manage through the FOMC release (Jul 28–29) rather than hold through it.
  • VIX spike. A VIX move above 25 would inflate the put premiums but also expand the expected move enough that the short strike may come into play. Watch the term ratio — a flip into backwardation (VIX > VIX3M) is the early warning.

Position management

  • Profit-take: Close at 50% of max profit, which is a debit of roughly $65 to close (you received ~$130, you give back ~$65, you keep ~$65).
  • Stop-loss: Close at 2× the credit received in loss — a debit of ~$260 to close. Beyond that, the structure is no longer asymmetric in your favor.
  • Time stop: Close by Wednesday, August 19 close (2 calendar days before the last trade day). AM-settled options lose their time value fast in the final week, and the Thursday Aug 20 last-trade-day means you cannot react on Friday morning.
  • Adjustment: If SPX drops within 1% of the short strike ($7,135 × 0.99 = $7,064) with 7+ calendar days remaining, roll the short strike down 5 points and out 1 week for a credit. Do not roll down without collecting a credit.
