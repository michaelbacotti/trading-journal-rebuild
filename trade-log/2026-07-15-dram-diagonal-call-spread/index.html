Why This Structure

A diagonal call spread with a back-month long lower strike and a front-month short higher strike is a thesis-shaped position: you're long time on a strike you want to own, and you're short theta on a strike you think is unlikely to be hit. The funding flows from the front-month's faster time decay into the back-month's slower one — netting you positive theta for the first ~120 days, while still keeping long upside exposure if DRAM rallies.

The 50-strike long back-month call is the directional bet. At $57.35 spot, the 50C is $7.35 OTM, but it's a January 2027 expiry — 184 DTE — so the position has six months to ride. Buying a deep-OTM LEAPS outright at this strike would cost about $17.50 per share today (the basis paid). On its own, that's $1,742.50 of capital at risk per contract, with the full amount eroding against theta in months 4–6 if DRAM stalls.

The 70-strike short front-month call is the financing leg. Selling the Dec 18 70C collects $9.05/share — about 52% of the long's cost — and that premium decays far faster than the long's premium does. Through the first ~150 days, theta on the short 70C exceeds theta on the long 50C, leaving the structure net-positive theta. After ~120 days the calculus starts to flip, but by that point the front-month is approaching expiry and the long 50C is still 184-DTE-style cheap.

The result is a 6-month bullish-to-neutral memory thesis where the structure does the theta harvesting for you, with explicit defined-risk cap at the debit paid. If DRAM spikes through 70 by Dec 18 the short side bleeds — but the long 50C continues to capture full intrinsic above 50 plus any remaining time value, and you can roll or close to recover.

Why DRAM, why now (again):

The diagonal adds a different shape — pure directional upside that does NOT cap at 90 — funded by a near-dated higher-strike sale.
  • The pullback. DRAM traded at ~$80+ in early June. By entry today it was at $57.35, down 6.5% on the day and roughly 28% off the recent high. Buying the 50C Jan '27 at $17.43 is meaningfully cheaper than buying it two weeks ago at the highs. The diagonal structure turns that lower entry into a wider profit zone (max profit $1,234.94 vs. the $1,207.50 on the July 13 condor, despite similar notional).
  • 88% IV. Elevated, but not peak — Optionstrat's range slider sits at 59% of its 1-year IV range. There's still vol premium to harvest on the front-month short.

Why diagonal over a naked long 50C LEAPS:

  • Naked 50C at $17.43 → $1,742.50 debit, full theta drag in months 4–6, no defined exit on the upside.
  • Diagonal structure → $837.50 debit, theta-positive for the first ~120 days, defined max loss.

Why diagonal over another LEAPS condor (like the 7/13 trade):

  • The condor caps upside at $90. The diagonal has uncapped upside above 70. If DRAM has a hard upcycle and runs through 80, 90, 100, the diagonal captures the full intrinsic above 50 (modulo the short 70C losses).
  • Different payoff shape. The condor is a "high-probability, modest-payoff" range bet; the diagonal is a "lower-probability, higher-payoff" directional bet.
  • Different time profile. The condor's profit zone starts to crystallize in month 4–5; the diagonal's peak profit comes when the front-month expires worthless and the back-month retains time value.

Why Dec 18 / Jan 15 '27 specifically (and not the standard Sep/Dec diagonal):

  • Dec 18 / Jan 15 is the closest standard expiration pair available with reasonable liquidity, and the 28-day gap between front-month and back-month is enough to harvest the theta curve differential.
  • Going further out (Mar '27 long) inflates the debit because the long leg's basis climbs faster than the short leg's. Staying in the Dec/Jan window keeps the funding efficient.

Intraday Setup (entry)

DRAM traded down to $57.35 on July 15, down 6.5% from the prior session — the largest single-day drop in the past month. The sell-off stemmed from a broader memory cycle de-rating: SK Hynix and Micron both pulled back 4-6% overnight on supply-chain chatter about HBM demand normalization, and the ETF (which holds both names plus Samsung, Kioxia, and Western Digital) re-priced with them. The 88% IV print on the front-month 70C reflected this volatility pickup.

The setup logic:

  1. Higher base rate. The July 13 condor already validated the thesis that DRAM has a $60-80 zone to grind through over the next 6 months. The diagonal adds a different shape to the same thesis.
  2. Cheaper LEAPS price. Buying the 50C Jan '27 today at $17.43 is meaningfully cheaper than two weeks ago when DRAM was at $80. Lower entry = wider profit zone.
  3. Theta curve. The Dec 18 / Jan 15 '27 window gives ~28 days of theta-differential harvesting — exactly the right scale for a 6-month structure.
  4. Overnight risk cheap. With the 70C short sitting $12.65 above spot, the short-side decay runs freely for the next ~120 days. If DRAM rallies hard, position size at 0.25% NLV prevents catastrophic damage.

The two legs were filled as a single multi-leg order at the open. The long 50C Jan '27 filled at $17.43 (mid $17.40, slippage tolerable). The short 70C Dec 18 filled at $9.05 (mid $9.10, credit). Net debit $8.38/share = $837.50 per spread.

The position was entered as a single spread (1 long + 1 short), sized within the playbook's 0.25% NLV per-trade cap. This is the second DRAM trade in the journal — the July 13 condor remains open and will continue to be tracked in its own entry.

Management Plan

  • Open through month 3 (Oct 2026): Do nothing. Theta on the short 70C is doing its job; long 50C is holding time value. Position is in the harvest zone.
  • Month 3–4 (Nov 2026): Begin watching delta closely. If DRAM is in the $55-65 zone, the position is approaching the front-month window — start thinking about closing the short 70C before expiry rather than rolling it.
  • Month 5 (Dec 1–18, 2026): Take action. Either close the short 70C at 50-70% of its initial credit AND continue holding the long 50C (turning the position into a naked long 50C LEAPS for the final 28 days), OR close the entire spread and redeploy the capital. Don't let the short 70C go into expiration if DRAM is anywhere above 60.
  • Month 5–6 (Dec 18, 2026 – Jan 15, 2027): Hold the long 50C naked (if rolled out of the short) until 30 DTE before Jan 15, then close.
  • Stop loss: 2× debit (≈ $1,675 position loss, DRAM would need to fall toward $40–45 and stay there), OR close if DRAM closes below $50 for 5+ sessions. The structure doesn't need to be defended to expiry — the defined risk is the debit.

Lessons

What worked (so far): Entering the diagonal on the same day as a -6.5% pullback. The pullback gives the 50C Jan '27 a structurally cheaper entry (lower delta, lower debit, lower breakeven than buying at the highs). Without the pullback, the same structure would still work but with a wider debit and lower max profit.

What I'd reconsider: The 70 strike on the short front-month. A 65C might have collected slightly less premium but given the long 50C more directional room — net theta capture would be similar but the breakeven would shift lower. The 70C was chosen because DRAM at 70 is a meaningful technical level, but a 5-point lower strike changes the shape of the payoff meaningfully.

Vol surface behavior at entry: The 88% IV print is mid-range for DRAM — it's been higher (95%+ in May) and lower (60% in late June). The diagonal benefits from holding positive vega, so the vol is a feature, not a bug, at entry. If DRAM vol mean-reverts toward 60% in the next 90 days the long 50C loses extrinsic faster than the short 70C gains it back, which is the front-loaded risk of the structure.

For the playbook: The diagonal is a useful complementary structure for LEAPS memory-cycle entries. Same memory thesis as the July 13 condor, but with different payout shape. Lower max-probability-of-success (the diagonal requires DRAM to either stay in a zone or rally), but higher upside if the rally comes. Worth considering as a "second strike" after an initial condor lands well.
