About the education section

The education section covers the foundational concepts that underpin every options strategy in the playbook. The pieces are organized into three clusters. The risk cluster covers expected value, risk-vs-reward asymmetry, and the math that makes a 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio the floor rather than the goal. The probability cluster covers option-implied versus historical probabilities, risk-neutral versus real-world probabilities, and how to use probability of profit (POP) without over-relying on it as a decision input. The math cluster covers standard deviations in options trading, how implied vol maps to expected moves, and why strike selection in a spread book should be sigma-anchored rather than delta-anchored.

Each piece is designed to be self-contained but cross-references the others. Readers new to options should start with the risk cluster (the conceptual underpinning), then move to the probability cluster (how to think about outcomes), then to the math cluster (the technical scaffolding). Readers who already trade options but want to deepen their understanding can read the pieces in any order, though the math cluster is most useful after the probability cluster because the math depends on the probability framing.

How the education pieces are kept current

Conceptual pieces are updated when the underlying market structure changes in a way that makes the original framing misleading. For example, the expected-value primer was updated in early 2026 when the CBOE changed its methodology for computing the expected move based on the VIX term structure. The probability primer was updated in late 2025 to reflect new academic research on the relationship between implied vol and realized vol across different volatility regimes.

We do not retire content that has aged out of usefulness. Instead, we add a header note explaining when the piece was last updated and what conditions it was written for. Readers can decide for themselves whether the framework still applies to current conditions. For pieces that are no longer accurate (because the underlying methodology or data source has changed substantially), we publish a new version with a clear note in the original explaining the replacement.

What the education section does not cover

The education section does not cover specific trade recommendations, broker mechanics, tax implications, or regulatory requirements. Those topics are out of scope for this site. Readers should consult a licensed tax adviser for tax questions, a licensed broker for account mechanics, and a licensed attorney for legal questions. The content here is educational material about how options work and how the strategies in the playbook are designed; it is not investment advice.
