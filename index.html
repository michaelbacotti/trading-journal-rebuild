About this site

The Trading Journal is a public record of one options trader's day-to-day work. Every entry includes the instrument traded, the strike, the expiration, the premium paid or received, the target, the stop, the reasoning behind the entry, and the actual outcome. Wins and losses are both on the record. The site is published by Dependability Holdings LLC, a Nevada limited liability company. The site is not a registered investment adviser, broker-dealer, or financial planner. Nothing on the site constitutes investment advice.

The core book is built around the S&P 500 (SPX) and the S&P 500 mini options (XSP), with selective exposure to sector ETFs (XLK, XLF, XLE, XLV, XLY, XLP, XLI, XLU, XLB, XLRE, XLC) when relative-value opportunities arise. The primary instrument is options spreads — vertical credit spreads, debit spreads, iron condors, calendars, and diagonals — chosen for defined risk, positive expected value, and time decay favorable to the position. The journal does not trade single-leg long options except in narrow, named circumstances where the structure is justified.

How to read the journal

Readers looking for the most recent trade should start at the trade log, which is updated in real time during market hours. Readers looking for the methodology should start at the playbook, which documents the standard operating procedure that governs every trade. Readers looking for the reasoning behind specific trades should start at the forecast section, which records the tactical view at the time each trade was entered. Readers looking to understand the underlying mechanics of the strategies should start at the education section.

Each trade journal entry links to the forecast entry that documented the rationale at entry time. Each forecast entry links to the playbook rules that govern the position size and management. Each strategy page in the strategy library documents the anatomy and behavior of the specific structure used. The site is designed so readers can move between sections and trace any trade from entry reasoning to outcome.

This site is not investment advice. It is not a recommendation to buy, sell, or hold any security. It is not a signal service.
